# Guardianship & Minor Terms

Load when has_minor_children flag detected.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Testamentary Guardian** | Person named in a will to raise minor children if parents die. The will is the only document where guardianship can be legally designated. |
| **Guardian of the Person** | Person responsible for raising children - making decisions about education, healthcare, religion, and daily life. |
| **Guardian of the Estate** | Person responsible for managing inherited assets for minor children. Can be same person as guardian of the person or different. |
| **Successor Guardian** | Backup guardian if primary guardian cannot serve. |
| **HEMS Standard** | Health, Education, Maintenance, and Support. Common standard giving trustees guidance on when to distribute from a trust. Considered a "safe" standard that limits distributions to genuine needs. |
| **Spendthrift Provision** | Trust clause protecting assets from beneficiary's creditors and poor decisions. Beneficiary cannot pledge or assign their trust interest. |
| **Children's Trust** | Subtrust created within main trust to hold and manage inheritance for minor beneficiaries until they reach specified age(s). |
| **Staggered Distribution** | Inheritance released in portions at different ages rather than all at once. Example: 1/3 at 25, 1/3 at 30, 1/3 at 35. |
| **Discretionary Trust** | Trust where trustee decides when and how much to distribute based on beneficiary's needs. Maximum protection but less predictable for beneficiary. |
| **Mandatory Distribution** | Distribution trustee must make regardless of circumstances. Example: "All income shall be distributed annually." |
| **Letter of Intent** | Non-binding document providing guidance on children's upbringing - education preferences, religious practices, health considerations, relationships with relatives. |
| **Age of Majority** | Age when person is legally an adult. 18 in most states. Trust provisions often delay distribution beyond this age. |
