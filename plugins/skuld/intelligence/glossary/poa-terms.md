# Power of Attorney Terms

Load when generating POA documents.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Power of Attorney (POA)** | Legal document authorizing someone (agent) to act on your behalf in specified matters. |
| **Principal** | Person who grants power of attorney. The person whose affairs the agent will manage. |
| **Agent** | Person authorized to act on principal's behalf under power of attorney. Also called "Attorney-in-Fact." |
| **Attorney-in-Fact** | Legal term for the agent under a power of attorney. Does not need to be a lawyer. |
| **Durable Power of Attorney** | POA that remains effective if principal becomes incapacitated. "Durable" means it survives incapacity. Essential for estate planning. |
| **Non-Durable Power of Attorney** | POA that terminates if principal becomes incapacitated. Rarely appropriate for estate planning. |
| **Springing Power of Attorney** | POA that only becomes effective upon a triggering event (usually incapacity). Harder to use in practice because incapacity must be proven. |
| **Immediate Power of Attorney** | POA effective immediately upon signing. Recommended over springing - easier to use when needed. |
| **Limited Power of Attorney** | POA granting authority for specific purpose or time period. Example: authority to sell a specific property. |
| **General Power of Attorney** | POA granting broad authority over principal's financial affairs. What most estate plans include. |
| **Financial Power of Attorney** | POA for financial matters - banking, investments, real estate, taxes. |
| **Healthcare Power of Attorney** | POA for medical decisions. Different document from financial POA in most states. |
| **Successor Agent** | Backup agent if primary agent cannot serve. |
| **Third Party Reliance** | Legal protection for banks and others who rely on POA in good faith. Important for POA acceptance. |
| **Revocation** | Canceling a power of attorney. Must be in writing and delivered to agent and third parties. |
