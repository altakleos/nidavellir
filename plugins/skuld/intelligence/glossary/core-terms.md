# Core Estate Planning Terms

These fundamental terms are loaded at session start.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Grantor** | Person who creates and funds a trust. Also called "Settlor" or "Trustor" in some states. |
| **Trustee** | Person or entity responsible for managing trust assets according to trust terms. |
| **Beneficiary** | Person who receives benefits from a trust, will, or other estate planning document. |
| **Successor Trustee** | Person who takes over as trustee if the original trustee can no longer serve. |
| **Pour-Over Will** | A will that directs any assets not in the trust at death to "pour over" into the trust. |
| **Probate** | Court-supervised process to validate a will and distribute assets. Can take months to years and involves court fees and public records. |
| **Intestate** | Dying without a valid will. Assets are distributed according to state law, which may not match your wishes. |
| **Fiduciary** | Person legally obligated to act in another's best interest. Trustees, executors, and agents are fiduciaries. |
| **Estate** | Everything a person owns at death - real estate, bank accounts, investments, personal property, etc. |
| **Decedent** | Legal term for a person who has died. |
| **Heir** | Person legally entitled to inherit under state law (if no will exists). |
| **Legatee/Devisee** | Person named in a will to receive property. Legatee receives personal property; devisee receives real estate. |
| **Residuary Estate** | Everything remaining after specific gifts and debts are paid. |
| **Codicil** | Amendment to a will. Must be executed with same formalities as the original will. |
