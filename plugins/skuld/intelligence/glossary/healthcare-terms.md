# Healthcare Terms

Load when generating healthcare directive documents.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Advance Directive** | General term for documents expressing healthcare wishes in advance of incapacity. Includes living wills and healthcare proxies. |
| **Living Will** | Document expressing end-of-life treatment preferences. States what medical treatments you want or don't want if terminally ill or permanently unconscious. |
| **Healthcare Proxy** | Document naming someone (your "agent") to make medical decisions when you can't. Also called Healthcare Power of Attorney or Medical Power of Attorney. |
| **Healthcare Agent** | Person you name to make medical decisions on your behalf. Should know your values and wishes. |
| **HIPAA** | Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act. Federal law protecting medical privacy. HIPAA authorization allows named people to access your medical records. |
| **HIPAA Authorization** | Document authorizing specific people to receive your protected health information. Without it, doctors may not share information even with family. |
| **DNR/DNAR** | Do Not Resuscitate / Do Not Attempt Resuscitation. Medical order (not legal document) directing that CPR not be performed. |
| **POLST/MOLST** | Physician Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment. Medical order signed by physician that EMS and hospitals must follow. Different from advance directive. |
| **Terminal Condition** | Incurable condition that will result in death within a relatively short time. |
| **Persistent Vegetative State** | Condition of permanent unconsciousness with no reasonable expectation of recovery. |
| **Comfort Care** | Medical care focused on pain relief and dignity rather than extending life. Also called palliative care. |
| **Life-Sustaining Treatment** | Medical interventions that prolong life, including ventilators, feeding tubes, dialysis, CPR. |
