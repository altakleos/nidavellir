# Durable Financial Power of Attorney - Template Structure

## Document Overview

A Durable Power of Attorney (POA) for finances allows a trusted person (agent or attorney-in-fact) to manage your financial affairs if you become incapacitated. "Durable" means it remains effective during incapacity.

## Template Purpose

This template provides the structural framework for generating educational financial POA documents. The agent will adapt sections based on:
- State statutory requirements
- Scope of powers needed
- Springing vs. immediate effectiveness
- Special circumstances

---

## DURABLE POWER OF ATTORNEY FOR FINANCES

### Principal Identification

> I, [PRINCIPAL NAME], currently residing at [ADDRESS], [City], [State] [ZIP], ("Principal"), appoint the following person(s) as my agent(s) to manage my financial affairs:

---

## Article I: Designation of Agent

### Primary Agent

> I designate [AGENT NAME] of [City, State] as my Agent (attorney-in-fact).

### Successor Agents

> If my first-named Agent is unable or unwilling to serve, I designate the following successors in order:
>
> First Successor: [NAME] of [City, State]
> Second Successor: [NAME] of [City, State]

### Co-Agents (Optional)

**Option A - Co-agents acting jointly**:
> My Agents must act together and agree on all decisions.

**Option B - Co-agents acting independently**:
> Either Agent may act independently without the other's consent.

**Option C - Co-agents for different functions**:
> [Agent 1] shall handle [real estate], and [Agent 2] shall handle [banking].

---

## Article II: Durability and Effective Date

### Durability Statement

> This power of attorney is DURABLE. It shall not be affected by my subsequent disability or incapacity. It shall remain in full force and effect until I revoke it in writing or until my death.

### Effective Date Options

**Option A - Immediately Effective** (Recommended):
> This power of attorney is effective immediately upon my signing.

**Option B - Springing Power**:
> This power of attorney shall become effective only upon a determination that I am incapacitated, as defined below.

### Incapacity Definition (for Springing POA)

> I shall be considered incapacitated when:
> - [One/Two] licensed physician(s) certify in writing that I am unable to manage my property and financial affairs, OR
> - A court of competent jurisdiction has determined I am incapacitated.

**Note**: Springing POAs can cause delays. Most practitioners recommend immediately effective POAs with trusted agents.

---

## Article III: General Grant of Authority

### Broad Grant of Power

> I grant my Agent FULL POWER AND AUTHORITY to act on my behalf in all financial matters, including but not limited to the specific powers listed below.

## Article IV: Specific Powers Granted

### Real Property Powers
- Buy, sell, lease, and exchange real estate
- Manage and maintain real property
- Execute deeds and title documents
- Mortgage and refinance real estate
- Collect rents and pay expenses
- Evict tenants and enforce leases

### Financial Institution Powers
- Access all bank and brokerage accounts
- Open and close accounts
- Deposit and withdraw funds
- Write checks and authorize transfers
- Manage safe deposit boxes
- Execute documents for financial institutions

### Investment Powers
- Buy, sell, and trade securities
- Manage investment portfolios
- Exercise stock options and rights
- Establish and manage retirement accounts
- Make investment decisions

### Business Operations Powers
- Operate and manage business interests
- Sign contracts and agreements
- Hire and fire employees
- Buy and sell business assets
- File business tax returns

### Insurance Powers
- Purchase and cancel insurance policies
- Change beneficiaries
- File claims and collect proceeds
- Maintain existing coverage

### Tax Powers
- Prepare and file tax returns
- Sign tax documents
- Make tax elections
- Represent before IRS and state agencies
- Collect refunds

### Government Benefits Powers
- Apply for government benefits
- Represent with Social Security, Medicare, Medicaid
- Collect benefit payments
- Appeal adverse decisions

### Estate Planning Powers
- Create, amend, or revoke trusts
- Fund trusts with my assets
- Change beneficiary designations
- Make gifts (subject to limitations)
- Disclaim inheritances

### Personal and Family Maintenance
- Pay personal expenses
- Maintain lifestyle
- Support dependents
- Pay for healthcare (coordinate with healthcare POA)

## Article V: Special Powers (Hot Powers)

### IMPORTANT: These powers often require specific authorization

### Gift-Making Authority

**State Requirements Vary**:
- Some states require specific grant
- New York: Requires Statutory Gifts Rider (SGR)
- Many states: Must be expressly stated

**Gift Power Language**:
> I authorize my Agent to make gifts of my property to any of the following:
> - My spouse
> - My descendants
> - Any person or charity I have previously supported
>
> Gifts in any calendar year shall not exceed the federal annual gift tax exclusion amount per recipient [OR specify dollar amount or percentage].

### Trust Modification Powers

> I authorize my Agent to:
> - Create, amend, or revoke trusts on my behalf
> - Add or remove beneficiaries from trusts
> - Fund my existing revocable trust

### Beneficiary Designation Changes

> I authorize my Agent to change beneficiary designations on:
> - Life insurance policies
> - Retirement accounts
> - Pay-on-death accounts
> - Any other beneficiary-designated assets

### Delegation Authority

> I [authorize / do not authorize] my Agent to delegate powers to another person.

---

## Article VI: Limitations on Authority

### Prohibited Actions

> My Agent shall NOT:
> - Create or change my Will
> - Vote in any election on my behalf
> - Change the designation of my healthcare agent
> - Make healthcare decisions (see separate Healthcare Directive)
> - Perform personal services requiring my unique skills

### Gift Limitations

> Any gifts made by my Agent shall not exceed $[amount] per person per year, or the federal annual gift tax exclusion, whichever is less.

### Self-Dealing Restrictions

> My Agent shall not engage in transactions that benefit the Agent personally unless such transactions are:
> - Specifically authorized below, OR
> - Approved by [named person or professional]

---

## Article VII: Agent's Duties and Standards

### Fiduciary Duties

> My Agent has a fiduciary duty to act:
> - In my best interest
> - In good faith
> - Within the scope of authority granted
> - With the care a prudent person would exercise

### Recordkeeping

> My Agent shall keep accurate records of all transactions made on my behalf and shall provide an accounting upon request by:
> - Me (if competent)
> - My guardian or conservator
> - [Named oversight person]

### Conflicts of Interest

> My Agent shall avoid conflicts of interest and self-dealing, except as specifically authorized in this document.

---

## Article VIII: Compensation and Reimbursement

### Agent Compensation

**Option A - No compensation**:
> My Agent shall serve without compensation.

**Option B - Reasonable compensation**:
> My Agent shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered.

**Option C - Specific compensation**:
> My Agent shall be entitled to compensation of $[amount] per [hour/month/year].

### Expense Reimbursement

> My Agent shall be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in performing duties under this power of attorney.

---

## Article IX: Third-Party Reliance

### Good Faith Reliance

> Any third party may rely upon the representations of my Agent regarding the authority granted by this document and shall not be liable for actions taken in good faith reliance.

### Liability Protection for Third Parties

> No financial institution, title company, or other person shall be liable for acting in good faith upon the instructions of my Agent under this power of attorney.

---

## Article X: Revocation and Termination

### Revocation

> I may revoke this power of attorney at any time by:
> - Written notice to my Agent
> - Executing a new power of attorney expressly revoking this one
> - Destroying this document with intent to revoke

### Termination Events

> This power of attorney terminates upon:
> - My death
> - My written revocation
> - Appointment of a guardian or conservator (unless court orders continuation)
> - [Other specified events]

---

## Article XI: Miscellaneous Provisions

### Governing Law
> This power of attorney shall be governed by the laws of [State].

### Severability
> If any provision is invalid, remaining provisions remain in effect.

### Photocopies
> A photocopy or electronic copy of this signed power of attorney shall have the same force and effect as the original.

### Prior Powers Revoked
> I revoke any prior durable power of attorney for finances I have executed.

### Principal Signature

```

[PRINCIPAL NAME], Principal
```

### Notary Acknowledgment

```

evidence) to be the person whose name is subscribed to this instrument,

```

### Witness Attestation (if required by state)

```
We declare that the Principal signed this document in our presence, appeared
to be of sound mind, and was not under duress, fraud, or undue influence.

____________________________    ____________________________
Witness 1 Signature              Witness 2 Signature
```

### Agent Acceptance (Recommended)

```
AGENT'S ACCEPTANCE

I, [AGENT NAME], accept appointment as Agent under this Power of Attorney.
I acknowledge that I have a fiduciary duty to the Principal.

Date: _______________

_________________________________
[AGENT NAME], Agent
```

## Educational Disclaimers

> **IMPORTANT**: This document is provided for educational purposes only. Power of attorney requirements vary significantly by state. Financial institutions may have specific acceptance requirements. An attorney licensed in your state should review and customize this document before execution.

> **WARNING**: A power of attorney grants significant authority over your finances. Choose your agent carefully. Consider naming someone you trust completely with your financial wellbeing.
