# Connecticut (CT) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** **Estate Tax** ($13.61M exemption - mirrors federal)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | 2 | Required | Both witnesses AND notary |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Advance directive |

## Key Features

### Estate Tax
- Exemption: $13.61 million (2025) - mirrors federal
- Maximum rate: 12%
- Unified with gift tax
- Previously had lower exemption - now aligned with federal

### Gift Tax
- Connecticut has state gift tax (unusual)
- Unified with estate tax
- Lifetime gifts count against exemption

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: life use of 1/3 of estate
- Outright share of 1/3 if no descendants
- Can elect statutory share

## Common Considerations
1. Gift tax planning (unique state gift tax)
2. Estate tax exemption now high but check current year
3. Probate courts by district - localized procedures
4. Two witnesses required for POA
