# Minnesota (MN) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** **Estate Tax** ($3M exemption)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory short form |
| Healthcare | 2 or Notary | Either acceptable | Healthcare directive |

## Key Features

### Estate Tax
- Exemption: $3 million (2025)
- Maximum rate: 16%
- Decoupled from federal
- Lower exemption requires more planning

### Minnesota Uniform Trust Code
- Modern trust provisions
- Trust protector allowed
- Decanting permitted

### Transfer on Death Deed
- Minnesota has TOD deed for real estate
- Avoids probate
- Revocable during lifetime

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: varies by length of marriage
- Up to 50% for marriages of 15+ years
- UPC-based augmented estate

## Common Considerations
1. $3M exemption lower than federal - tax planning needed
2. TOD deed available for real estate
3. Healthcare directive flexible (witnesses OR notary)
4. Cold weather - plan for out-of-state healthcare needs
