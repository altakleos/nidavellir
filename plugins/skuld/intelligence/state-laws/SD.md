# South Dakota (SD) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law (with Community Property Trust option)
**State Tax:** None (no income tax either)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory form available |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Healthcare directive |

## Key Features

### No State Death Taxes
- No estate tax
- No inheritance tax
- No state income tax
- Very tax-friendly state

### Premier Trust Jurisdiction
South Dakota is considered one of the best trust jurisdictions:
- **Dynasty trusts**: No rule against perpetuities
- **Asset protection trusts (DAPT)**: Strong protection
- **Directed trusts**: Comprehensive directed trust statute
- **Privacy**: Trust details not public record
- **Community Property Trust**: Available for basis step-up

### Community Property Trust Option
- Married couples can elect community property treatment
- Provides double step-up in basis benefit
- Available since 2016

### Transfer on Death Deed
- South Dakota has TOD deed for real estate
- Avoids probate
- Revocable during lifetime

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: varies by length of marriage
- Up to 50% for marriages of 15+ years
- UPC-based augmented estate

## Common Considerations
1. Premier trust jurisdiction - consider SD trust situs
2. No state income tax on trust income
3. Community property trust for step-up benefits
4. Dynasty trust planning
5. Many out-of-state residents use SD trusts
