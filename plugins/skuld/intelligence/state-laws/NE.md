# Nebraska (NE) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** **Inheritance Tax** (county-level collection)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory form available |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Power of attorney for healthcare |

## Key Features

### Inheritance Tax (Unique Structure)
- **Immediate family**: Spouse exempt; children/parents 1% over $100,000
- **Remote relatives**: 11% over $40,000
- **Others**: 15% over $25,000
- Collected at county level
- Nebraska is one of few states with inheritance tax

### Age of Majority: 19
- Nebraska is one of only two states where majority is 19 (with Alabama)
- Affects witness requirements

### Transfer on Death Deed
- Nebraska has TOD deed for real estate
- Avoids probate
- Revocable during lifetime

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: varies by length of marriage
- Up to 50% for marriages of 15+ years
- UPC-based augmented estate

## Common Considerations
1. Inheritance tax - plan for non-immediate family beneficiaries
2. Age of majority is 19 (unusual)
3. TOD deed available
4. Agricultural/farm succession planning important
