# Washington (WA) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Community Property
**State Tax:** **Estate Tax** ($2.193M exemption)

| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Healthcare directive |

## Key Features

### Community Property State
- Property acquired during marriage is community
- Each spouse owns 50%
- Double step-up in basis at death
- No quasi-community property rules (unlike CA)

### Estate Tax
- Exemption: $2.193 million (2025 - indexed)
- Maximum rate: 20%
- One of higher top rates in nation
- Lower exemption affects many families

### Death with Dignity Act
- Washington has medical aid in dying
- Death with Dignity Act (2009)
- Specific requirements and documentation

### Transfer on Death Deed
- Washington has TOD deed for real estate
- Avoids probate
- Revocable during lifetime

### No State Income Tax
- No personal income tax
- But has estate tax
- Trust income not taxed at state level

## Elective Share
- No traditional elective share (community property state)
- Community property protections instead

## Common Considerations
1. Community property state - double step-up available
2. Estate tax with $2.193M exemption - planning important
3. Seattle area property values often exceed exemption
4. Death with Dignity considerations
5. Tech industry wealth - stock option and RSU planning
