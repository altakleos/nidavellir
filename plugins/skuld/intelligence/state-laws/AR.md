# Arkansas (AR) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** None

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory form available |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Declaration or POA |

## Key Features

### No State Death Taxes
- No estate tax
- No inheritance tax
- Only federal taxes apply

### Dower and Curtesy
- Arkansas retained modified dower/curtesy
- Surviving spouse has life estate in 1/3 of real property
- In addition to statutory share

### Elective Share
- Surviving spouse can elect against will
- Complex calculation involving children and property types

## Common Considerations
1. Dower/curtesy affects real estate transfers during lifetime
2. Spousal signature may be needed on deeds
3. Consider trust to avoid probate complications
4. Healthcare directive allows living will or healthcare POA
