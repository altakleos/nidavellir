# Georgia (GA) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 1 (Full Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**Population Rank:** 8
**State Tax:** None (no state estate or inheritance tax)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving affidavit available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | 2 | Required | Both witnesses AND notary required |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Specific statutory form |

## Key Features

### No State Death Tax
- Georgia has no estate tax
- Georgia has no inheritance tax
- Only federal estate tax applies
- Favorable for high-net-worth individuals

### Year's Support
Georgia has unique surviving family protection:
- Surviving spouse and minor children entitled to "year's support"
- Takes priority over all debts except secured debts
- Amount determined by court based on previous standard of living
- Must be applied for within 24 months
- Superior to elective share in many cases

## Power of Attorney

### Georgia Financial Power of Attorney Act
- Two witnesses required
- Notarization required
- Specific statutory form recommended (O.C.G.A. ยง 10-6B-70)
- Durable unless stated otherwise

### Key Provisions
- Hot powers (gifts, beneficiary changes) must be specifically granted
- Agent must sign acceptance before acting
- Financial institutions can request certification of validity

### Springing POA
- Georgia allows springing powers
- Must specify triggering condition
- Requires physician certification of incapacity

## Healthcare Documents

### Georgia Advance Directive for Health Care
- Combines living will and healthcare agent designation
- Two witnesses required (specific disqualifications)
- Notarization not required
- Statutory form in O.C.G.A. ยง 31-32-4

### Witness Restrictions for Healthcare Directive
Witnesses cannot be:
- Healthcare agent
- Healthcare provider
- Employee of healthcare provider
- Owner/operator of healthcare facility

### POST (Physician Orders for Scope of Treatment)
- Georgia's version of POLST
- Physician orders for seriously ill
- Printed on distinctive form

## Probate

### Solemn Form Probate
- Full probate with notice to all heirs
- Provides more finality
- Cannot be challenged after certain period

### Common Form Probate
- Simplified without formal notice
- Can be challenged for 4 years
- Faster but less certainty

### No Administration Necessary
- Estate consists only of exempt property
- Value insufficient to require administration
- Affidavit filed with probate court

### Small Estate Affidavit
- No real estate involved
- Estate value under certain threshold
- Simplified transfer process

## Special Considerations

### Georgia Trust Code
- Comprehensive trust law
- Trust protector provisions
- Decanting allowed
- Nonjudicial settlement agreements permitted

### Tenancy by the Entirety
- Available only for married couples
- Provides creditor protection
- Property passes automatically to survivor
- Must be specifically created

### Homestead Exemption
- Various exemptions based on age, disability, income
- Must apply with county tax commissioner
- Basic exemption for all homeowners
- Enhanced for seniors 62+ and 65+

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Forgetting year's support** - Powerful protection often overlooked
2. **Using non-statutory POA form** - May face acceptance issues
3. **Missing witness requirements** - POA needs both witnesses AND notary
4. **Solemn vs. common form confusion** - Different notice and finality implications
5. **Healthcare directive witness restrictions** - Specific disqualifications apply

## Transfer on Death Instruments

### TOD Registration
- Securities can have TOD beneficiaries
- Bank accounts with POD designation
- Avoids probate for these assets

### Real Estate
- Georgia does NOT have TOD deed statute
- Must use trust or joint ownership for real estate probate avoidance
- Lady Bird deeds not recognized

## Witness Requirements

Witnesses for will must:
- Be 14+ years old (lower than most states)
- Be competent
- Sign in presence of testator
- Disinterested recommended but not required

Witnesses for POA:
- Must be 18+
- Two required
- Plus notarization required

Witnesses for healthcare directive:
- Must be 18+
- Two required
- Specific disqualifications (see above)
