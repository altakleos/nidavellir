# Ohio (OH) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 1 (Full Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**Population Rank:** 7
**State Tax:** None (estate tax repealed 2013)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving NOT allowed | Witnesses may need to testify in probate |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory form available |
| Healthcare | N/A | Notarization OR 2 witnesses | Either method valid |

## Key Features

### No State Estate Tax
- Ohio repealed estate tax effective January 1, 2013
- Only federal estate tax applies
- Previously had $338,333 exemption (very low)
- Repeal simplifies planning significantly

### Spousal Elective Share
Ohio uses an augmented estate approach:
- Surviving spouse can elect against will
- **Net estate formula**:
  - If children from current marriage only: 1/2 if one child, 1/3 if two+ children
  - If children from prior relationship: 1/3 minimum
  - If no children: 1/2 of estate
- Includes some nonprobate transfers in calculation

## Power of Attorney

### Statutory Form
- Ohio Revised Code ยง 1337.60
- Notarization required
- No witness requirement
- Durable by default unless stated otherwise
- Immediate effectiveness recommended

### Agent Powers
- Specific enumeration recommended
- Gift-making authority must be explicit
- Real estate powers should be clearly stated

## Healthcare Documents

### Healthcare Power of Attorney
- Can use notarization OR two witnesses (not both required)
- Names healthcare agent (called "attorney in fact")
- Separate from financial POA

### Living Will Declaration
- Ohio has statutory form
- Two witnesses OR notary
- Applies to terminal condition or permanently unconscious state
- Can include nutrition/hydration preferences

### DNR Comfort Care
- Ohio has statewide DNR protocol
- Requires physician signature
- Separate from living will

## Probate

### Full Administration
- Supervised by probate court
- Required for estates over small estate limits
- Executor called "fiduciary"

### Summary Release from Administration
- Personal property under $35,000
- Real estate can be released by surviving spouse up to $40,000

### Release from Administration
- Total assets under $100,000
- Surviving spouse as sole beneficiary
- Simplified process

### Transfer on Death Designation Affidavit
- Available for real estate (ORC ยง 5302.22)
- Avoids probate for real property
- Popular planning tool in Ohio

## Special Considerations

### Transfer on Death (TOD) Deed
- Ohio was early adopter
- Real estate passes outside probate
- Revocable during lifetime
- Very commonly used

### Ohio Trust Code
- Modern trust provisions
- Trust protector allowed
- Decanting permitted
- Virtual representation for beneficiaries

### Medicaid Estate Recovery
- Ohio recovers from estates for Medicaid paid
- Planning needed for long-term care
- 5-year lookback period

### Homestead Exemption
- Property tax reduction for seniors 65+ and disabled
- Income-based qualification
- Must apply with county auditor

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using outdated forms** - Ohio updated POA laws in 2012
2. **Forgetting TOD deed benefits** - Simple probate avoidance tool
3. **Not understanding elective share** - Complex calculation in Ohio
4. **Healthcare directive ambiguity** - Either witnesses OR notary, not both required
5. **Assuming old estate tax rules** - Repealed in 2013

## Witness Requirements

Witnesses for will must:
- Be 18+ years old
- Be competent
- Sign in presence of testator
- Not need to be disinterested (though recommended)

Witnesses for healthcare directive:
- Must be 18+
- Cannot be spouse
- Cannot be attending physician
- Cannot be administrator of nursing facility
