# Colorado (CO) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** None

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Attestation recommended |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory form available |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Advance directive or proxy |

## Key Features

### No State Death Taxes
- No estate tax
- No inheritance tax
- Tax-friendly state

### Colorado Uniform Trust Code
- Modern trust provisions
- Trust protector allowed
- Decanting permitted
- Directed trusts available

### Transfer on Death Deed
- Colorado has TOD deed (beneficiary deed)
- Avoids probate for real estate
- Revocable during lifetime

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: 50% of augmented estate
- Based on UPC model
- Includes nonprobate assets

## Common Considerations
1. Use beneficiary deed for real estate probate avoidance
2. Strong trust laws make Colorado favorable for trusts
3. Medical aid in dying law (End of Life Options Act)
4. Coordinate healthcare directive with MAID provisions
