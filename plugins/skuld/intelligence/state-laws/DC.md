# District of Columbia (DC) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** **Estate Tax** ($4.71M exemption)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving NOT allowed | Witnesses may need to testify in probate |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory form available |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Advance directive |

## Key Features

### Estate Tax
- Exemption: $4.71 million (2025 - indexed)
- Maximum rate: 16%
- Decoupled from federal
- Lower exemption than federal

### Not a State
- District of Columbia is not a state
- Federal district with unique status
- Has its own probate and tax systems
- DC Superior Court handles probate

### Tenancy by the Entirety
- Available for real property
- Married couples only
- Creditor protection

### Uniform Trust Code
- DC adopted UTC
- Modern trust provisions

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: varies by family composition
- 1/3 or 1/2 of net estate
- Complex calculation based on issue and property

## Common Considerations
1. Estate tax at $4.71M - lower than federal
2. Coordinate with MD and VA for metro residents
3. Federal employees - coordinate with federal benefits
4. International residents - embassy and diplomatic considerations
5. High property values in DC
