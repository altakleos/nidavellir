# Oregon (OR) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** **Estate Tax** ($1M exemption - VERY LOW)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory form available |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Advance directive |

## Key Features

### Estate Tax - VERY LOW EXEMPTION
- Exemption: **$1 million** (lowest in nation)
- Maximum rate: 16%
- Decoupled from federal
- **CRITICAL**: Affects many Oregon families

### Tax Structure
- Graduated rates 10%-16%
- Natural resource credit available
- Farm/forestland special valuation available

### Transfer on Death Deed
- Oregon has TOD deed for real estate
- Avoids probate
- Revocable during lifetime

### Death with Dignity Act
- Oregon was first state with medical aid in dying
- Specific requirements and documentation

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: 1/4 of net estate
- Plus homestead rights

## Common Considerations
1. **$1M exemption catches many families** - planning essential
2. Portland metro property values often exceed threshold
3. Farm/forestland valuation may reduce tax
4. Coordinate with WA if Clark County/Portland area
5. Death with Dignity considerations for healthcare planning
