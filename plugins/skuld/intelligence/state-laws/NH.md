# New Hampshire (NH) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** None (no income tax on wages either)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | 2 | Required | Witnesses AND notary |
| Healthcare | 2 or Notary | Either acceptable | Advance directive |

## Key Features

### No State Death Taxes
- No estate tax
- No inheritance tax
- No state income tax (on wages/salary)
- Tax-friendly state

### Asset Protection Trust (DAPT)
- New Hampshire allows domestic asset protection trusts
- Self-settled spendthrift trusts
- Favorable trust laws

### Transfer on Death Deed
- New Hampshire does NOT have TOD deed for real estate
- Must use trust or joint ownership

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: dower/curtesy rights
- Life estate in 1/3 of real property
- Or $10,000 plus homestead rights

## Common Considerations
1. No state death taxes
2. POA requires both witnesses AND notary
3. No TOD deed - use trust for real estate
4. Favorable trust jurisdiction
5. Many Boston-area residents reside in NH
