# Nevada (NV) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Community Property
**State Tax:** None (no income tax either)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory form available |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Durable POA for healthcare |

## Key Features

### Community Property State
- Property acquired during marriage is community
- Each spouse owns 50%
- Double step-up in basis at death
- Community Property with Right of Survivorship available

### No State Death Taxes
- No estate tax
- No inheritance tax
- No state income tax
- Very tax-friendly state

### Asset Protection Trust (DAPT)
- Nevada allows domestic asset protection trusts (since 1999)
- 2-year fraudulent transfer lookback (one of shortest)
- Self-settled spendthrift trusts
- Directed trustee provisions

### Dynasty Trust
- No rule against perpetuities for trusts
- Trusts can last perpetually
- Popular jurisdiction for dynasty planning

## Elective Share
- No traditional elective share (community property state)
- Community property protections instead

## Common Considerations
1. Community property state - double step-up available
2. Excellent asset protection trust laws
3. Dynasty trust jurisdiction (no perpetuities)
4. Many California residents consider Nevada trusts
5. Gaming industry considerations if applicable
