# Tennessee (TN) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 1 (Full Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**Population Rank:** 16
**State Tax:** None (no income tax, no estate tax)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving affidavit available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | 2 | Required | Both witnesses AND notary |
| Healthcare | 2 or Notary | Either acceptable | Advance care plan form |

## Key Features

### No State Death Tax
- Tennessee has no estate tax (repealed 2016)
- Tennessee has no inheritance tax (phased out by 2016)
- No state income tax (Hall Tax on investment income ended 2021)
- Very tax-friendly state

### Elective Share
Surviving spouse entitled to elective share:
- If no minor children: **40% of net estate**
- If minor children: **40% of net estate** (same percentage)
- Includes augmented estate
- Must elect within 9 months (extensions possible)

## Power of Attorney

### Durable Power of Attorney
Tennessee requires formal execution:
- Two witnesses required
- Notarization required
- Statutory form available (T.C.A. ยง 34-6-109)

### Key Features
- Durable unless otherwise stated
- Immediate effectiveness recommended (not springing)
- Agent should sign acceptance

### Powers Requiring Express Grant
- Making gifts
- Creating trusts
- Changing beneficiary designations
- Delegating authority
- Transactions with agent

## Healthcare Documents

### Advance Care Plan (Healthcare POA)
Tennessee combines documents into "Advance Care Plan":
- Healthcare agent designation
- Living will provisions
- HIPAA authorization
- Two witnesses OR notarization (not both required)

### Statutory Form
- Comprehensive statutory form (T.C.A. ยง 68-11-1803)
- Covers healthcare agent, treatment preferences, organ donation
- Widely accepted form

### Witness Requirements (if not notarized)
Two witnesses required who are:
- 18+ years old
- Not related by blood or marriage
- Not entitled to any part of estate
- Not financially responsible for healthcare

### POST (Physician Orders for Scope of Treatment)
- Tennessee's POLST equivalent
- Medical orders for seriously ill patients
- Signed by physician
- Distinctive form

## Probate

### Common Form Probate
- Simplified procedure
- No formal notice to heirs required
- Can be challenged

### Solemn Form Probate
- Formal procedure with notice
- Provides more finality
- Requires citation to interested persons

### Small Estate Affidavit
- Personal property under $50,000
- 45-day waiting period
- Affidavit filed with court

### Summary Administration
- Simplified for qualifying estates
- Less court involvement

## Special Considerations

### Tennessee Investment Services Act (Dynasty Trusts)
- Tennessee allows perpetual trusts
- No rule against perpetuities for trusts
- Favorable trust jurisdiction
- Asset protection trust provisions (2007)

### Tennessee Asset Protection Trust
- Domestic asset protection trust (DAPT) available
- Self-settled spendthrift trusts allowed
- Must meet specific requirements
- 2-year (good faith) to 4-year fraudulent transfer lookback

### Tenancy by the Entirety
- Available for married couples
- Real and personal property
- Strong creditor protection (even from one spouse's creditors)
- Automatic right of survivorship

### Homestead Exemption
- $5,000 value (one of lowest in nation)
- Additional exemption for surviving spouse and minor children
- Must be designated in writing to claim

### Community Property Trust Option
Tennessee allows married couples to opt into community property:
- Tennessee Community Property Trust Act (2010)
- Creates community property for trust assets
- Provides step-up in basis benefit at first death
- Unique planning opportunity

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Assuming old inheritance tax rules** - Fully repealed by 2016
2. **Not utilizing community property trust** - Unique TN advantage
3. **Low homestead exemption** - Only $5,000 protected
4. **Springing POA difficulties** - Immediate recommended
5. **Healthcare advance directive flexibility** - Either witnesses OR notary
6. **Dynasty trust opportunities missed** - No perpetuities rule for trusts

## Transfer on Death Instruments

### TOD Deed
- Tennessee does NOT have TOD deed statute
- Must use trust or other methods for real estate

### TOD Securities
- Standard TOD registration available
- Bank POD accounts allowed
- Vehicles can have TOD beneficiary

## Witness Requirements

Witnesses for will must:
- Be 18+ years old
- Be competent
- Sign in presence of testator
- Disinterested recommended

Witnesses for POA:
- Must be 18+
- Two required
- Plus notarization required

Witnesses for advance care plan (if not notarized):
- Must be 18+
- Two required
- Not related by blood or marriage
- Not beneficiary
- Not responsible for healthcare costs
