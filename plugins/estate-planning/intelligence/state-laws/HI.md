# Hawaii (HI) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** **Estate Tax** ($5.49M exemption)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory form available |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Advance healthcare directive |

## Key Features

### Estate Tax
- Exemption: $5.49 million (2025)
- Lower than federal exemption
- Graduated rates up to 20%
- One of highest top rates in nation

### Hawaii Uniform Trust Code
- Modern trust provisions
- Trust protector allowed
- Decanting permitted

### Tenancy by the Entirety
- Available for real and personal property
- Creditor protection

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: 1/3 of net estate
- Or intestate share, whichever is larger
- Augmented estate includes nonprobate assets

## Common Considerations
1. State estate tax starts at $5.49M - affects more estates
2. High top rate (20%) - tax planning important
3. Out-of-state property complications (many HI residents have mainland assets)
4. Probate through circuit court
