# New Jersey (NJ) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** **Inheritance Tax** (Class C & D beneficiaries only)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | 2 | Required | Witnesses AND notary |
| Healthcare | 2 | Recommended | Advance directive + proxy |

## Key Features

### No Estate Tax (Repealed 2018)
- New Jersey repealed its estate tax as of January 1, 2018
- Previously had $675,000 exemption (very low)

### Inheritance Tax (Still in Effect)
- **Class A (exempt)**: Spouse, children, parents, grandchildren
- **Class C**: Siblings, daughter/son-in-law (11%-16% after $25,000)
- **Class D**: Others (15%-16% after $500)
- Class B abolished

### Tenancy by the Entirety
- Available for real property
- Creditor protection

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: 1/3 of augmented estate
- Includes nonprobate assets
- Complex calculation

## Common Considerations
1. Estate tax repealed but inheritance tax remains
2. Inheritance tax affects siblings and unrelated beneficiaries
3. POA requires both witnesses AND notary
4. Coordinate with NY if working in NYC area
5. High property values - asset protection planning
