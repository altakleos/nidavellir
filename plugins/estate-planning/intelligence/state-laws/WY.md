# Wyoming (WY) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 2 (Standard Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**State Tax:** None (no income tax either)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Statutory form available |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Advance healthcare directive |

## Key Features

### No State Death Taxes
- No estate tax
- No inheritance tax
- No state income tax
- Very tax-friendly state

### Favorable Trust Jurisdiction
Wyoming has become a popular trust jurisdiction:
- **Dynasty trusts**: 1000-year rule (effectively perpetual)
- **Asset protection trusts (DAPT)**: Available
- **Directed trusts**: Comprehensive provisions
- **Privacy**: Strong privacy protections
- **Low costs**: No state trust income tax

### Transfer on Death Deed
- Wyoming has TOD deed for real estate
- Avoids probate
- Revocable during lifetime

### Homestead
- Homestead exemption up to $40,000 in value
- Plus 160 acres or 1/4 acre in town

## Elective Share
- Surviving spouse: 1/2 of estate (no children) or 1/4 (with children)
- Plus homestead rights

## Common Considerations
1. Excellent trust jurisdiction - consider WY situs
2. No state income tax on trust income
3. Large ranches and agricultural property
4. Mineral rights and oil/gas considerations
5. Many out-of-state residents use WY trusts
