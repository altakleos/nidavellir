# Alabama (AL) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 1 (Full Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**Population Rank:** 24
**State Tax:** None (no state estate or inheritance tax)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Self-proving affidavit available | Standard execution |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | 1 | Required | Witness AND notary required |
| Healthcare | 2 | Not required | Advance directive for healthcare |

## Key Features

### No State Death Tax
- Alabama has no estate tax
- Alabama has no inheritance tax
- Only federal estate tax applies
- Tax-friendly state for wealth transfer

### Elective Share
Surviving spouse can elect against will:
- **Dower/Curtesy abolished** - now statutory elective share
- **Greater of**:
  - All estate if no children
  - First $50,000 + 1/2 of remaining estate if decedent had children
  - Intestate share if larger
- Must elect within 6 months

## Power of Attorney

### Durable Power of Attorney
Alabama requires:
- One witness (minimum)
- Notarization required
- Statutory form available

### Key Provisions
- Durable unless stated otherwise
- Effective immediately unless springing
- Agent should sign acceptance
- Banks must accept valid POA (with protections)

### Powers Requiring Specific Authorization
- Making gifts
- Creating/amending trusts
- Designating beneficiaries
- Transactions benefiting agent

## Healthcare Documents

### Advance Directive for Health Care
Alabama combines living will and healthcare proxy:
- Two witnesses required
- Notarization NOT required
- Statutory form (Ala. Code ยง 22-8A-4)
- Can separate agent designation from treatment wishes

### Witness Requirements
Witnesses cannot be:
- Under 19 years old (Alabama's age of majority is 19)
- The healthcare proxy (agent)
- Related by blood or marriage
- Entitled to any portion of estate
- Provider of healthcare

### DNR Order
- Separate from advance directive
- Physician order
- Applies to resuscitation specifically

## Probate

### Full Administration
- Probate court in county of residence
- Letters testamentary issued to executor
- Standard supervised administration

### Summary Distribution
- Estates under $25,000 in personal property
- Simplified procedure
- 30-day waiting period

### Small Estate Affidavit
- Very small estates
- No formal administration
- Affidavit procedure

### Muniment of Title
- Alabama does NOT have muniment of title
- Must use full or summary administration

## Special Considerations

### Alabama Uniform Trust Code
- Adopted modern trust code
- Trust protector provisions
- Decanting allowed
- Nonjudicial modification available

### Age of Majority
- Alabama: **19 years old** (unusual)
- Affects witness requirements
- Affects guardianship termination
- One of only two states with 19 (Nebraska is other)

### Tenancy by the Entirety
- Available for married couples (real property)
- Creditor protection benefits
- Automatic right of survivorship

### Homestead Exemption
- $15,000 value (160 acres rural, 1/2 acre urban)
- Plus property tax exemption for primary residence
- Additional exemptions for seniors and disabled

### Pretermitted Heirs
- Children born after will may take intestate share
- Strong protection for after-born children
- Will should specifically address

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Age of majority is 19** - Witnesses must be 19+, not 18
2. **Witness restrictions for healthcare directive** - Comprehensive exclusions
3. **POA needs both witness AND notary** - Missing either problematic
4. **No muniment of title** - Must go through administration
5. **Elective share calculation** - Complex with $50,000 floor

## Transfer on Death Instruments

### TOD Deed
- Alabama does NOT have TOD deed statute
- Must use trust or joint ownership for real estate

### TOD Securities
- Standard TOD registration available
- Bank POD accounts allowed

### Real Estate Planning
- Trust most effective for real estate probate avoidance
- Joint tenancy with right of survivorship (but careful of gift implications)
- Enhanced life estate deed (Lady Bird) NOT recognized

## Witness Requirements

Witnesses for will must:
- Be 19+ years old (Alabama's age of majority)
- Be competent
- Sign in presence of testator
- Disinterested recommended

Witnesses for POA:
- Must be 19+
- One required minimum
- Plus notarization required

Witnesses for advance directive:
- Must be 19+
- Two required
- Cannot be healthcare proxy
- Cannot be related
- Cannot be beneficiary
- Cannot be healthcare provider
