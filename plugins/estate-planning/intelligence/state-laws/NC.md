# North Carolina (NC) - Estate Planning Requirements

**Tier:** 1 (Full Documentation)
**Property Type:** Common Law
**Population Rank:** 9
**State Tax:** None (estate tax decoupled from federal)

## Execution Requirements

| Document | Witnesses | Notarization | Special Requirements |
|----------|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Will | 2 | Required for self-proving | Attested will standard |
| Trust | N/A | Required | Notary acknowledgment |
| POA | N/A | Required | Witness not required but recommended |
| Healthcare | 2 | Required | Both witnesses AND notary |

## Key Features

### No State Estate Tax
- North Carolina decoupled from federal estate tax (2013)
- No state estate or inheritance tax
- Only federal estate tax applies
- Previously had estate tax with $5.25M exemption

### Elective Share
Surviving spouse can claim elective share:
- **Intestate share** if no will, OR
- **Elective share**: varies by length of marriage
  - Less than 5 years: 15% of total net assets
  - 5-10 years: 25%
  - 10-15 years: 33%
  - 15+ years: 50%
- Includes augmented estate (some nonprobate assets)

## Power of Attorney

### Statutory Short Form POA
- North Carolina has comprehensive statutory form
- Notarization required
- Witnesses not required but recommended
- Durable unless stated otherwise

### Key Features
- Can be immediate or springing
- Gift-making must be specifically authorized
- Banks must accept valid POA (penalties for refusal)
- Registration option for real estate powers

### Important Provisions
- Agent acceptance recommended
- Successor agents should be named
- Special instructions section available

## Healthcare Documents

### Health Care Power of Attorney
- Names healthcare agent
- Two witnesses AND notary required
- Specific statutory form (N.C.G.S. ยง 32A-25.1)
- Can include treatment preferences

### Advance Directive for Natural Death (Living Will)
- Separate from healthcare POA
- Two witnesses AND notary required
- Applies to terminal and incurable conditions
- Specific statutory form available

### Witness Restrictions
Witnesses cannot be:
- Related by blood or marriage
- Heir or beneficiary
- Attending physician
- Employee of attending physician

### MOST (Medical Orders for Scope of Treatment)
- North Carolina's POLST equivalent
- Physician orders for seriously ill
- Portable medical orders

## Probate

### Estate Administration Types

#### Independent Administration
- Clerk of Superior Court supervises
- Less court involvement than some states
- Executor/administrator appointed

#### Summary Administration
- Estates under $30,000 (without real estate)
- Estates under $20,000 (with real estate)
- Simplified procedure

#### Collection by Affidavit
- Personal property under $20,000
- No administration opened
- 30-day waiting period

## Special Considerations

### North Carolina Uniform Trust Code
- Modern trust code adopted
- Trust protector provisions
- Decanting permitted
- Nonjudicial modification available

### Tenancy by the Entirety
- Available for married couples (real property only)
- Creditor protection benefits
- Automatic right of survivorship

### Homestead Exemption
- $35,000 for homestead (real property)
- Additional personal property exemptions
- Bankruptcy-specific exemptions
- Must elect NC exemptions in bankruptcy

### Real Property Considerations
- Recording requirements for deeds
- Documentary stamp tax on conveyances
- $1 per $500 of value

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Healthcare directive complexity** - Requires BOTH witnesses AND notary
2. **Elective share calculation** - Marriage length affects percentage
3. **Witness restrictions for healthcare** - Related parties disqualified
4. **Tenancy by entirety** - Only available for real property
5. **Out-of-state documents** - May not meet NC formal requirements

## Transfer on Death Instruments

### TOD Securities Registration
- Allowed under NC Uniform TOD Security Registration Act
- Avoids probate for securities

### POD Bank Accounts
- Payable on death designations allowed
- Passes outside probate

### Real Estate
- North Carolina does NOT have TOD deed statute
- Must use trust or other methods for real estate probate avoidance

## Witness Requirements

Witnesses for will must:
- Be 18+ years old
- Be competent
- Sign in presence of testator
- Not need to be disinterested (though recommended for will contests)

Witnesses for healthcare documents:
- Must be 18+
- Two required
- Cannot be related or beneficiary
- Cannot be attending physician or their employee

Notarization required for:
- Self-proving affidavit (will)
- Power of attorney
- Healthcare POA
- Advance directive for natural death
