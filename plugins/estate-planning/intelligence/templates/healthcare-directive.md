# Healthcare Directive Package - Template Structure

## Document Overview

A comprehensive healthcare directive package typically includes three integrated documents:
1. **Healthcare Power of Attorney** (Healthcare Proxy) - Names an agent for medical decisions
2. **Living Will** (Advance Directive) - States treatment preferences
3. **HIPAA Authorization** - Authorizes disclosure of medical information

## Template Purpose

This template provides the structural framework for generating educational healthcare directive documents. The agent will adapt sections based on:
- State-specific requirements and terminology
- Treatment preferences
- Religious or personal values
- Family situation

---

# PART 1: HEALTHCARE POWER OF ATTORNEY

## Purpose

Designates an agent (healthcare proxy, healthcare representative, patient advocate) to make medical decisions when you cannot speak for yourself.

---

### Principal Identification

> I, [PRINCIPAL NAME], currently residing at [ADDRESS], [City], [State] [ZIP], appoint the following person as my healthcare agent:

---

## Article I: Designation of Healthcare Agent

### Primary Agent

> I designate [AGENT NAME] of [City, State] as my Healthcare Agent.
>
> Phone: [Number]
> Email: [Email]
> Address: [Address]

### Successor Agents

> If my first-named Healthcare Agent is unable, unwilling, or unavailable to serve, I designate:
>
> First Successor: [NAME] of [City, State]
> Phone: [Number]
>
> Second Successor: [NAME] of [City, State]
> Phone: [Number]

---

## Article II: Grant of Authority

### General Authority

> My Healthcare Agent has full authority to make any healthcare decision I could make if I had capacity, including but not limited to:
>
> - Consent to or refuse medical treatment
> - Choose healthcare providers and facilities
> - Access medical records and information
> - Authorize admission to or discharge from facilities
> - Make decisions about pain management
> - Make decisions regarding life-sustaining treatment
> - Make anatomical gift decisions
> - Authorize autopsy

### When Agent's Authority Begins

**Option A - Incapacity Only** (Default in most states):
> My Healthcare Agent's authority becomes effective when my attending physician determines I lack capacity to make healthcare decisions.

**Option B - Immediate Authority**:
> My Healthcare Agent's authority is effective immediately, but I retain the right to make my own decisions while I have capacity.

---

## Article III: Specific Instructions to Agent

### Treatment Preferences

> I want my Healthcare Agent to consider these preferences:
>
> **Pain Management**: I want my pain controlled even if it might hasten my death.
>
> **Quality of Life**: [Principal's statement about quality of life values]
>
> **Religious/Spiritual Values**: [If applicable]
>
> **Specific Treatments**: [Any specific preferences about blood transfusions, etc.]

### Guidance for Agent

> In making decisions, my Healthcare Agent should:
> - First, try to determine what I would want based on my known values
> - Consider my written statements in this document and my living will
> - Make the decision that is in my best interest if my wishes are unclear

---

## Article IV: Limitations (if any)

### Optional Limitations

> I limit my Healthcare Agent's authority as follows:
> - [Specific limitations, if any]
> - [OR: I do not impose any limitations]

### Decisions Agent CANNOT Make

> My Healthcare Agent cannot:
> - Consent to my commitment to a mental health facility (in most states)
> - Consent to psychosurgery
> - [Other state-specific limitations]

---

## Article V: Additional Provisions

### Conflict Resolution

> If there is disagreement between my Healthcare Agent and family members, my Healthcare Agent's decision controls.

### Revocation

> I may revoke this Healthcare Power of Attorney at any time by:
> - Notifying my Healthcare Agent orally or in writing
> - Executing a new Healthcare Power of Attorney
> - Any other act demonstrating intent to revoke

---

# PART 2: LIVING WILL (ADVANCE DIRECTIVE FOR HEALTHCARE)

## Purpose

States your wishes about end-of-life treatment when you cannot communicate and have a terminal condition or are permanently unconscious.

---

## Declaration

> I, [DECLARANT NAME], make this Living Will to state my wishes about end-of-life medical treatment.

---

## Article I: Conditions When This Applies

### Terminal Condition

> This Living Will applies if I have a **terminal condition** - an incurable illness or injury from which there is no reasonable probability of recovery and which will result in my death.

### Permanent Unconsciousness

> This Living Will also applies if I am in a state of **permanent unconsciousness** - a condition in which I am unaware of myself or my environment, from which I am not expected to recover.

### Other Conditions (some states)

> This Living Will applies if I have an **end-stage condition** - an irreversible condition caused by injury, disease, or illness that has resulted in severe and permanent deterioration indicated by incompetency and complete physical dependency.

---

## Article II: Treatment Preferences

### Life-Sustaining Treatment

**Option A - Withhold/Withdraw**:
> If I am in a terminal condition or permanently unconscious, I direct that life-sustaining treatment be withheld or withdrawn and that I be permitted to die naturally.

**Option B - Trial Period**:
> I want a trial period of life-sustaining treatment of [number] days to allow time for recovery or for family to gather. If no improvement, then withhold/withdraw.

**Option C - All Measures**:
> I want all available medical treatment to keep me alive as long as possible, regardless of my condition.

### Specific Treatments

#### Artificial Nutrition and Hydration

**Option A - Withhold**:
> I do NOT want artificial nutrition (feeding tubes) or hydration (IV fluids) if I am terminally ill or permanently unconscious.

**Option B - Time-Limited**:
> I want artificial nutrition and hydration for a trial period of [number] days, then reassess.

**Option C - Continue**:
> I DO want artificial nutrition and hydration to continue.

#### Mechanical Ventilation

**Option A - Withhold**:
> I do NOT want to be placed on a mechanical ventilator if I am terminally ill or permanently unconscious.

**Option B - Trial Period**:
> I want mechanical ventilation for a trial period of [number] days.

**Option C - Continue**:
> I DO want mechanical ventilation.

#### Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)

**Option A - No CPR**:
> I do NOT want CPR attempted if my heart stops.

**Option B - CPR with Limits**:
> I want CPR attempted, but if not successful after reasonable effort, do not continue.

**Option C - Full CPR**:
> I DO want all CPR measures attempted.

#### Dialysis

> I [do / do not] want kidney dialysis if I am terminally ill or permanently unconscious.

---

## Article III: Comfort Care

### Palliative Care Statement

> I want to receive comfort care at all times, including:
> - Pain medication (even if it may hasten death)
> - Treatment for anxiety and agitation
> - Hygiene and positioning
> - Hydration for comfort (ice chips, swabs)
> - Spiritual support

---

## Article IV: Organ and Tissue Donation

### Anatomical Gift Preferences

**Option A - Donate all**:
> Upon my death, I wish to donate any organs, tissues, and eyes that are medically suitable for transplantation, therapy, research, or education.

**Option B - Donate with limitations**:
> I wish to donate the following only: [specify organs/tissues]

**Option C - Do not donate**:
> I do NOT wish to be an organ or tissue donor.

### Maintain Life Support for Donation

> I understand that life support may be maintained briefly after my death to facilitate organ donation.

---

## Article V: Pregnancy Clause

### Treatment During Pregnancy (if applicable)

**Option A - Maintain life support**:
> If I am pregnant, I want life-sustaining treatment continued in an attempt to allow the fetus to develop and be born.

**Option B - Apply wishes regardless**:
> My wishes apply regardless of whether I am pregnant.

**Option C - Viability-based decision**:
> If the fetus is viable, maintain life support; if not viable, apply my wishes.

---

# PART 3: HIPAA AUTHORIZATION

## Purpose

Authorizes healthcare providers to share your protected health information with designated individuals.

---

## Authorization Statement

> I, [PRINCIPAL NAME], authorize my healthcare providers to release and disclose my protected health information to the following individuals:

---

## Authorized Persons

### Full Access

> The following persons may receive ANY information about my health condition, treatment, and prognosis:
>
> 1. [NAME] - [Relationship] - Phone: [Number]
> 2. [NAME] - [Relationship] - Phone: [Number]
> 3. My Healthcare Agent named in my Healthcare Power of Attorney

### Limited Access (Optional)

> The following persons may receive LIMITED information as specified:
>
> [NAME] may receive information about: [specify, e.g., "general condition only"]

---

## Scope of Authorization

> This authorization covers:
> - All medical records, past and present
> - Mental health records (unless separately restricted)
> - HIV/AIDS status
> - Substance abuse treatment records (unless separately restricted)
> - Genetic information

---

## Duration

> This authorization remains in effect:
> - Until I revoke it in writing, OR
> - Upon my death, my personal representative may access records

## Witness Restrictions by State

### Common Restrictions (Most States)
Witnesses generally CANNOT be:
- The healthcare agent
- Your attending physician
- An employee of your healthcare provider (some exceptions)

### Stricter States
Some states add:
- Cannot be related by blood or marriage
- Cannot be entitled to any part of your estate
- Cannot be financially responsible for your care

## Educational Disclaimers

> **IMPORTANT**: This document is provided for educational purposes only. Healthcare directive requirements vary significantly by state. Some states have specific statutory forms that are recommended or required. An attorney or healthcare provider in your state should review and customize this document.

> **COMMUNICATION**: Discuss your healthcare wishes with your Healthcare Agent, family members, and physicians. Provide copies to your healthcare providers for your medical record.

> **UPDATES**: Review and update your Healthcare Directive after major life events (marriage, divorce, serious illness) or at least every 5 years.
