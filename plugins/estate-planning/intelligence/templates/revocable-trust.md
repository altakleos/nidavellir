# Revocable Living Trust - Template Structure

## Document Overview

A revocable living trust (RLT) is the cornerstone of modern estate planning. It avoids probate, provides incapacity protection, and allows for sophisticated asset distribution.

## Template Purpose

This template provides the structural framework for generating educational revocable trust documents. The agent will adapt sections based on:
- State-specific requirements
- Family situation
- Asset types
- Distribution preferences

---

## Article I: Trust Name and Parties

### Key Elements
- **Trust Name**: "[GRANTOR NAME] Revocable Living Trust dated [DATE]"
- **Grantor(s)**: Person(s) creating the trust
- **Trustee(s)**: Initially the grantor(s)
- **Successor Trustee(s)**: Named in priority order
- **Beneficiaries**: Listed with full legal names

### State Variations
- **Community Property States**: Both spouses typically co-grantors
- **Common Law States**: Can be joint or separate trusts
- **Louisiana**: Must follow Civil Code requirements

## Article II: Trust Property

### Funding Provisions
- Schedule A: Real Property
- Schedule B: Financial Accounts
- Schedule C: Personal Property
- Schedule D: Business Interests

### Pour-Over Integration
- Reference to pour-over will

### State-Specific Considerations
- **California**: Transfer tax exemption for trust funding

## Article III: Rights Reserved by Grantor

### Standard Reserved Rights
- Right to revoke or amend
- Right to withdraw property
- Right to receive income and principal
- Right to add or remove beneficiaries

### Tax Provisions

## Article IV: Distributions During Lifetime

### To Grantor
- All income
- Principal as requested
- Health, education, maintenance, support (HEMS)

### To Others
- Gift provisions (if desired)
- Support of dependents
- Charitable distributions

---

## Article V: Incapacity Provisions

### Definition of Incapacity
**Option A - Two Physicians**:
> The Grantor shall be deemed incapacitated if two licensed physicians certify in writing that the Grantor is unable to manage their financial affairs.

**Option B - Single Physician + Family**:
> The Grantor shall be deemed incapacitated if one licensed physician and [specified family member] certify in writing...

**Option C - Court Determination**:
> Only upon a court finding of incapacity...

### Successor Trustee Powers
- Manage all trust assets
- Pay for grantor's care
- Continue distributions to dependents
- Maintain grantor's estate plan

### HIPAA Authorization
- Access to medical records
- Communication with healthcare providers

---

## Article VI: Administration After Death

### Initial Administration
- Pay debts and expenses
- File necessary tax returns
- Collect all trust assets

### Distribution to Surviving Spouse
**Option A - Outright Distribution**:
> All trust property passes outright to surviving spouse.

**Option B - Marital Trust (QTIP)**:
> All income to spouse for life, principal for HEMS, remainder to children.

**Option C - Survivor's Trust + Marital Trust**:
> Divide into shares based on estate tax planning needs.

### Distribution to Children
**Option A - Outright at Specific Age**:
> Distribute outright when child reaches age [25/30/35].

**Option B - Staged Distribution**:
> 1/3 at 25, 1/2 of remainder at 30, balance at 35.

**Option C - Lifetime Trust**:
> Hold in trust for child's lifetime for asset protection.

## Article VII: Trust for Minor or Young Adult Beneficiaries

### Trustee Powers for Minor's Trust
- Accumulate or distribute income
- Principal for HEMS
- Education distributions (define scope)

### Distribution Standards
```
MANDATORY: Health, Education, Maintenance, Support (HEMS)
```

### Age Thresholds
- Access to income: [Age 21-25]
- Partial principal: [Age 25-30]
- Full distribution: [Age 30-35]

## Article VIII: Special Needs Trust Provisions

### Third-Party SNT Language
> Any share passing to a beneficiary who is receiving or may receive means-tested government benefits shall be held in a Special Needs Trust...

### Key Provisions
- Supplemental, not supplanting, purpose
- Discretionary distributions only
- No right to demand distributions
- Trustee's sole and absolute discretion

### Remainder Beneficiaries
- Name alternate beneficiaries
- No payback required (third-party SNT)

---

## Article IX: Trustee Provisions

### Successor Trustee Designation
1. First Successor: [Name]
2. Second Successor: [Name]
3. Third Successor: [Name or Institution]

### Trustee Powers
- Invest and reinvest
- Buy, sell, and lease property
- Borrow money and mortgage assets
- Employ professionals
- Make tax elections
- Distribute in kind or cash

### Trustee Compensation
**Option A**: Reasonable compensation
**Option B**: Corporate trustee fee schedule
**Option C**: No compensation for family members

### Bond Waiver
> No trustee shall be required to post bond.

---

## Article X: Trust Protector (Optional)

### Trust Protector Powers
- Modify trust for tax law changes
- Add or remove beneficiaries
- Change situs (jurisdiction)
- Remove and appoint trustees
- Approve certain distributions

### Limitations
- Cannot benefit protector personally
- Cannot modify grantor's intent
- Subject to fiduciary duties

---

## Article XI: General Provisions

### Spendthrift Clause
> No beneficiary may assign, anticipate, or encumber their interest in this trust, and such interest shall not be subject to the claims of creditors.

### Rule Against Perpetuities
**Standard Savings Clause**:
> This trust shall terminate no later than 21 years after the death of the last survivor of [named measuring lives] living at the time of the Grantor's death.

**Dynasty Trust Option** (where permitted):
> This trust may continue for the maximum period permitted by the laws of [State].

### Governing Law
> This trust shall be governed by the laws of [State].

### Severability
> If any provision is invalid, remaining provisions remain in effect.

---

## Article XII: Definitions

### Key Definitions
- **Descendants**: Per stirpes vs. per capita
- **Education**: Define scope (undergraduate, graduate, vocational)
- **Incapacity**: Reference Article V definition
- **Issue**: Include or exclude adopted persons

---
