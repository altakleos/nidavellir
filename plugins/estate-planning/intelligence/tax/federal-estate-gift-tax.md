# Federal Estate and Gift Tax Overview

## 2025 Estate Tax Parameters

| Parameter | 2025 Value |
|-----------|------------|
| **Basic Exclusion Amount (BEA)** | $13,990,000 per person |
| **Married Couple (with portability)** | $27,980,000 combined |
| **Maximum Tax Rate** | 40% |
| **Annual Gift Exclusion** | $19,000 per recipient |
| **Gift Splitting (married)** | $38,000 per recipient |

## 2026 and Beyond - TCJA Expiration Update

### One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBB) - 2025

**CRITICAL UPDATE:** The 2025 OBBB Act made the increased exemption PERMANENT.

**Impact:**
- The $10 million base (indexed) is now permanent law
- **No sunset to ~$7 million in 2026**
- Exemption continues to increase with inflation annually
- Long-term planning is now more stable

### 2026 Projected Exemption
- Approximately **$15,000,000** per person (estimated with inflation)
- **$30,000,000** per married couple with portability

## Gift Tax

### Annual Exclusion Gifts
- **2025 Amount:** $19,000 per recipient per year
- No limit on number of recipients
- Not reported on gift tax return (below exclusion)
- Does not reduce lifetime exemption

### Lifetime Gift Tax Exemption
- **Unified with estate tax exemption**
- Same $13.99M (2025) applies to gifts and estate
- Taxable gifts reduce available estate exemption
- Gift tax paid at same 40% rate

### Gifts Not Subject to Tax
- Annual exclusion amounts
- Tuition paid directly to institution
- Medical expenses paid directly to provider
- Gifts to spouse (unlimited marital deduction)
- Gifts to qualified charities

### Gift Tax Return (Form 709)
**Must file when:**
- Gifts exceed annual exclusion to any recipient
- Gift-splitting election (married couples)
- Gifts of future interests
- Gifts to 529 plans exceeding 5x annual exclusion

## Estate Tax

### What's Included in Gross Estate
- All property owned at death
- Life insurance on decedent's life (if owned or incidents of ownership)
- Revocable trust assets
- Joint property (full value with non-spouse, 50% with spouse)
- Retirement accounts
- Gifts within 3 years (for certain purposes)
- Powers of appointment

### Deductions from Gross Estate
- **Marital deduction**: Unlimited transfers to surviving spouse
- **Charitable deduction**: Unlimited to qualified charities
- **Debts and expenses**: Mortgages, administration costs
- **State death taxes**: Deductible for federal

## Portability

### What is Portability?
Surviving spouse can use deceased spouse's unused exclusion (DSUE).

**Requirements:**
- Must be married at death
- Executor must file Form 706 (even if no tax due)
- Must elect portability on timely filed return
- Only applies to last deceased spouse

### Portability vs. Credit Shelter Trust

| Factor | Portability | Credit Shelter Trust |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Simplicity | Simpler | More complex |
| Asset protection | None | Some protection possible |
| State estate tax | May not help | May preserve state exemption |
| Growth | Growth in survivor's estate | Growth outside survivor's estate |
| Remarriage | Lost if survivor remarries | Protected |
| Basis step-up | At first death | Only at first death |

**Best practice:** Consider filing Form 706 for portability even if also using trust planning.

## Generation-Skipping Transfer (GST) Tax

### What is GST Tax?
Additional 40% tax on transfers to "skip persons" (generally grandchildren or lower).

### GST Exemption
- **Same as estate exemption**: $13.99M (2025)
- Allocated differently than estate exemption
- NOT portable (unlike estate exemption)

### Types of GST Transfers
- **Direct skip**: Gift directly to grandchild
- **Taxable termination**: Trust ends, passes to skip person
- **Taxable distribution**: Distribution from trust to skip person

### GST Planning
- Allocate GST exemption to trusts for descendants
- "GST-exempt trust" = no GST tax on future distributions
- Dynasty trusts leverage GST exemption for multiple generations

## Key Filing Requirements

### Form 706 (Estate Tax Return)
**Required when:**
- Gross estate + adjusted taxable gifts > $13.99M (2025)
- Portability election desired (regardless of estate size)

**Due:** 9 months after death (6-month extension available)

### Form 709 (Gift Tax Return)
**Required when:**
- Gifts exceed annual exclusion
- Gift-splitting
- Gifts to 529 exceeding 5x annual exclusion
- Gifts of future interests

**Due:** April 15 following year of gift (extension available)

## State Death Tax Credit vs. Deduction

**Historical:** Prior to 2005, federal credit for state death taxes effectively made state taxes "free."

**Current:** Only a **deduction** (not credit) for state death taxes. States with death taxes now represent additional cost.

## Common Planning Strategies

### Lifetime Gifting
- Use annual exclusion systematically
- Consider larger gifts using lifetime exemption
- Pay tuition/medical directly (unlimited)
- Gift appreciating assets (freeze value)

### Trust Planning
- Credit shelter trusts (A-B trusts)
- QTIP trusts for blended families
- Irrevocable life insurance trusts (ILIT)
- Grantor retained annuity trusts (GRAT)
- Charitable trusts (CRT, CLT)

### Charitable Planning
- Outright bequests (unlimited deduction)
- Charitable remainder trusts
- Charitable lead trusts
- Donor advised funds
- Private foundations
