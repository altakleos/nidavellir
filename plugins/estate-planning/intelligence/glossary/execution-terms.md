# Document Execution Terms

Load during Phase 6 (document execution guidance).

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Execution** | Legal term for properly signing a document. Requirements vary by document type and state. |
| **Self-Proving Affidavit** | Notarized statement by witnesses eliminating need for witness testimony at probate. Strongly recommended where allowed. |
| **Attestation Clause** | Statement at end of will where witnesses affirm they observed proper signing and believe testator was competent. |
| **Witness** | Person who watches another sign a document and then signs as witness. Cannot be a beneficiary in most states. |
| **Disinterested Witness** | Witness with no financial interest in the document. Required for wills in most states. |
| **Acknowledgment** | Notary statement that signer appeared, presented ID, and signed voluntarily. Used for trusts, POAs, deeds. |
| **Jurat** | Notary statement that signer took an oath regarding document contents. Less common than acknowledgment. |
| **Notarization** | Process of having notary public verify identity and witness signature. Does not verify document contents. |
| **Remote Online Notarization (RON)** | Notarization via video conference using approved platform. Available in most states for most documents. |
| **Signing Ceremony** | Coordinated event where all documents are signed with proper witnesses and notary. Recommended approach. |
| **Testamentary Capacity** | Mental ability to understand what you own, who your heirs are, and what signing a will means. Lower standard than for contracts. |
| **Undue Influence** | Pressure that overcomes person's free will. Common will contest ground. Having independent witnesses helps prevent claims. |
| **In Terrorem Clause** | No-contest clause penalizing beneficiaries who challenge document. Enforceability varies by state. |
