# Estate Administration Terms

Load during executor/administrator discussion.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Executor** | Person named in will to administer the estate through probate. Also called Personal Representative in many states. |
| **Personal Representative** | Modern term for executor used in many states. Appointed by court to administer estate. |
| **Administrator** | Person appointed by court to administer estate when there's no will (intestate) or no named executor. |
| **Letters Testamentary** | Court document authorizing executor to act on behalf of estate. Banks and other institutions require this. |
| **Letters of Administration** | Court document authorizing administrator when there's no will. |
| **Probate** | Court-supervised process of validating will, paying debts, and distributing assets. Public process with associated costs and delays. |
| **Summary Probate** | Simplified probate process for small estates. Thresholds vary by state (often $100,000-$200,000). |
| **Affidavit of Heirship** | Document used in some states to transfer property without full probate for small estates. |
| **Inventory** | List of estate assets that executor must file with court. Becomes public record. |
| **Creditor Claims Period** | Time period (usually 3-6 months) when creditors can file claims against estate. Executor must notify known creditors. |
| **Final Accounting** | Detailed report executor files with court showing all estate transactions. Beneficiaries can object. |
| **Estate Checking Account** | Bank account opened by executor to manage estate funds during administration. |
| **Fiduciary Bond** | Insurance policy protecting estate from executor misconduct. Often waived in will. |
| **Ancillary Probate** | Additional probate proceeding in state where decedent owned real estate but didn't reside. |
| **Notice to Creditors** | Published notice informing potential creditors of death and deadline to file claims. |
